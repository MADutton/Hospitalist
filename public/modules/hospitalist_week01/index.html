Lesson (12–15 min)

Key Concepts

1) What is a veterinary hospitalist?

Hospitalists own longitudinal inpatient care over hours to days. Their role is distinct from emergency triage and from intensive care specialists managing ventilators or vasopressors.

  • Own inpatient reassessment and monitoring
  • Recognize deterioration early
  • Coordinate ER → ward → discharge transitions
  • Optimize inpatient medical management

2) Scope boundaries

Included responsibilities:

  • Ward-level oxygen, fluids, electrolytes
  • Antimicrobial stewardship
  • Monitoring plans and deterioration thresholds
  • Step-down planning and discharge readiness

Excluded responsibilities:

  • Primary ER triage ownership
  • Primary ICU ventilator management

3) Time-horizon model

  • Emergency medicine: minutes → hours
  • Hospitalist medicine: hours → days

4) Mastery for Week 1

  • Create a prioritized inpatient problem list
  • Define deterioration triggers
  • Design a monitoring plan
  • Recognize when escalation is required
